In Our Work Together (Clinical Approach)

I hold space for what surfaces from lived experiences, supporting people in healing from painful or overwhelming moments. My approach integrates:

  • Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR)
  • Internal Family Systems (IFS)
  • Lifespan Integration (LI)
  • Polyvagal-Informed and Nervous System Exploration
  • Attachment-Informed Therapy

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR)

EMDR is a body and brain-informed trauma therapy. It works by accessing distressing memories in a context of safety and attunement, allowing the nervous system to integrate experiences that were previously fragmented or frozen in survival mode. During EMDR, you hold one foot in the past (the memory) and one foot in the present (safety and connection), creating a co-regulated space where the body and mind can update the story of what happened. The healing comes not from eye movements alone, but from the attuned, relational, and neurobiological process that supports regulation, integration, and growth.

Internal Family Systems (IFS)

IFS helps explore the different “parts” of self that may hold trauma or protective patterns. By connecting with these parts in a compassionate, non-judgmental way, you can develop self-leadership and internal safety.

Lifespan Integration (LI)

A gentle, body-based therapy that uses a client’s visual timeline of life memories to safely revisit and integrate past experiences with the present. This process helps the nervous system reset, resolve old trauma, and foster a coherent sense of self.

My approach is body-centered, trauma-informed, and anti-oppressive, grounded in polyvagal and attachment theory. These frameworks invite nervous system exploration, helping you notice how stress, trauma, and relationships show up in your body. I seek to understand people in the context of biology, trauma, and family systems, supporting connection with yourself and others to foster confidence, well-being, and resilience. While we cannot control external events or the actions of others, we can choose how we respond—and by looking inward, we can transform the impact of the past and create new possibilities.

Education & Training

As a Registered Clinical Counsellor (RCC) with the BC Association of Clinical Counsellors (BCACC), I bring a blend of professional experience and deep human connection to every person I work with. Before entering private practice, I spent 15 years working in public education, public health, frontline mental health, and substance use services—experience that deeply shapes my relational, trauma-informed approach to counselling.

  • Master of Arts in Clinical Counselling (MA)
  • Registered Clinical Counsellor (RCC) – BC Association of Clinical Counsellors (BCACC)
  • Bachelor of Arts in Child and Youth Care Counselling (CYCC)
  • Trauma & Crisis: ASIST, CPI Level II, Therapeutic Crisis Intervention
  • Addiction & Substance Use: Addiction Care and Treatment (ACTOC), Naloxone trained
  • Cultural Safety & Inclusion: San’yas, Indigenous Cultural Safety Hummingbird (VCH)
